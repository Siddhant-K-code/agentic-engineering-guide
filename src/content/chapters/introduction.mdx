> *"The best way to predict the future is to build the infrastructure for it."*

This book is for engineering leaders navigating the shift to AI-assisted
development. It covers the decisions, trade-offs, and organizational
patterns that determine whether AI agents become a force multiplier or a
source of burnout for your team.

The ideas here come from production experience - building agent
infrastructure, maintaining open-source authorization systems, and
talking to hundreds of engineers about what works and what doesn't. The
goal isn't to teach you a framework. It's to give you a way of thinking
about agents that survives the next model release.

The book is organized in ten parts that follow the natural progression
of agent adoption. Parts I-III cover foundations - what agents are, what
they can do, and how to secure them. Parts IV-V cover infrastructure -
the protocols, standards, and observability systems that production
agents need. Parts VI-VII cover orchestration and team practices - how
agents work together and how humans work with agents. Parts VIII-X cover
production deployment - the workflows, checklists, and playbooks that
take you from experimentation to production.

Each chapter is self-contained - you can read them in any order. But
they build on each other, and the cross-references between chapters
reflect real dependencies. If you're starting from scratch, read
linearly. If you're looking for answers to specific questions, use the
table of contents and cross-references to find the relevant chapters.

**Who this is for:** Engineering leaders, senior engineers, and
platform architects who are adopting AI agents - or deciding whether to.
You should be comfortable with software engineering concepts
(distributed systems, API design, CI/CD, observability) but you don't
need prior experience with AI or machine learning.

**What this is not:** A coding tutorial. A vendor comparison. A
prompt engineering guide. This is a book about engineering judgment in
the age of AI agents. It covers the decisions you need to make, the
trade-offs you need to understand, and the organizational patterns that
determine success or failure.

**What's different about this book:** Most AI books focus on the
technology - how models work, how to write prompts, how to use
frameworks. This book focuses on the engineering discipline - how to
build infrastructure, how to manage risk, how to organize teams, and how
to measure impact. The technology changes every month. The engineering
principles endure.

**How to use this book:** Read it once for the big picture, then
keep it as a reference. When you're setting up agent authorization,
re-read Chapters 8-10. When you're debugging a cost spike, re-read
Chapters 15 and 28. When you're onboarding a new team, hand them the
adoption playbook (Chapter 33) and the templates (Appendix D). The
cross-references between chapters are designed to help you navigate -
follow them when you need deeper context on a specific topic.

**A note on currency:** This book was written in February 2026.
Model capabilities, pricing, and benchmarks change rapidly. The specific
numbers in this book (benchmark scores, pricing tables, adoption
statistics) will be outdated within months. The principles, patterns,
and practices will remain relevant for years. When a specific number
matters for your decision, verify it against the current data. When a
principle matters, trust the book.
